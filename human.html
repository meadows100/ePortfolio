Professional Skills Matrix

Action Plan

In my Individual Reflection, I specified I would like to improve my proficiency in using GitHub. This is a very useful collaborative coding tool, which is also used at my workplace. Although I do not use it in my role, I do feel it would benefit me if I were to understand exactly how to get the most out of it. I plan to improve my knowledge by watching some short courses on LinkedIn Learning or YouTube, for example. I do not code, and so I do not see any time in the future where I will need to design an application. For this, I do not currently feel the need to learn any more about Flask, unless I did it for fun. However, I do see more value in improving my knowledge around Python. Again, I do not have any plans, however if I find a need to create a function or app which will increase my life in some way, I will definitely look to choose Python. I plan to improve my skills by simply practicing, and there is more than sufficient content available online whenever I get stuck with a problem.

Learning Loop

This helps you to tie up your learning across the modules, for a holistic perspective of the programme. You should then be able to apply it to your organisation’s work/project, where applicable. The loop should also include a review of Skills Matrices and action plans from individual modules. You could share it with the Head of Computing at the end of your programme as part of your final feedback on the course.
